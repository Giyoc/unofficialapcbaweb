WHAT IS HOME ECONOMICS?

The Home Economics (HE) strand offers various specializations that can lead to livelihood projects at home. This strand aims to give you job-ready skills that can help you in finding the right employment. Subjects in the Home Economics can also lead to National certifications based on TESDA standards.

If you are really not sure if you will be able to pursue any degree after Senior High or if you want to earn money immediately after graduation, then this track is for you. Even without college, the Technical-Vocational-Livelihood track will equip you with job-ready skills in the future.

This track also invests primarily on skills that can gain you requisite COCs (Certificates of Competency) and NCs (National Certifications) which would be essential when looking for better career opportunities in agriculture, electronics, and trade. This is also important when applying abroad where the skills you gain would prepare you as you join the workforce.

Home Economics is a field of study that focuses on practical skills and knowledge related to managing a household and improving the quality of life. It covers various areas such as:

    1.Cooking and Nutrition: Teaching individuals how to prepare and cook nutritious meals, as well as understanding the principles of balanced diets.
    2.Textiles and Clothing: Involves skills like sewing, fabric care, and understanding clothing construction.
    3.Family and Consumer Sciences: Explores topics related to family relationships, child development, personal finance, and resource management.
    4.Home Management: Covers skills like budgeting, interior design, and organization to efficiently run a household.

Home Economics aims to empower individuals with practical life skills that contribute to their well-being and the well-being of their families. While it may have evolved over the years and may be referred to by different names, the core focus remains on enhancing domestic and personal capabilities.
